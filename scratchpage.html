Koto: traditional musical instrument

The KOTO is a traditional Japanese stringed instrument with a rich history dating back centries. It's characterized by its long, gently curved body and 13 or 17 strings, each tuned to a specific pitch.

History

Koto, a revered instrument with a rich history, has been captivating audiences for centuries. Originating in ancient China, koto made its way to Japan during the Nara period, where it evolved into a cherished symbol of Japanese musical tradition. With its distinctive sound and elegant form, koto has played a central role in shaping Japan's musical landscape, from traditional court music to contemporary compositions.
